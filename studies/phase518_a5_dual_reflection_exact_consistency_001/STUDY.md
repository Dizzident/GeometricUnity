# Phase518: A5 Dual-Reflection Exact Consistency

Phase518 is a deterministic candidate-only census of the site-centered and
link-centered axis-0 reflection maps on the committed lattice-canonical `n=4`
periodic Kuhn complex. It counts exact vertex, oriented-edge, face, and
four-simplex closure and preserves explicit missing-image witnesses.

The executed terminal is
`a5-dual-reflection-exact-consistency-dual-candidate-oriented-complex-nonclosure`.
For each candidate, 2,048 of 3,840 edges and 3,072 of 12,800 faces map back
into the complex, while none of the 6,144 four-simplices does. Both vertex maps
remain exact bijective involutions, and the mapped restrictions pass their
twice-identity controls.

The phase distinguishes a vertex involution from a total oriented-complex
automorphism. Twice-identity on the mapped restriction cannot be promoted to
full closure, and missing images may never be silently represented by zero.

The final preregistration contract was frozen before Phase517 consumption and
exact-binds its summary, contract, program, and both candidate artifacts. Each
standalone candidate is required to equal its Phase517 summary row, and its
summary artifact path and hash must equal the Phase518 binding. The axis,
offset, map, slice partition, boundary rule, formal-only role,
non-registration status, and A5-validation firewall are parsed before the map
is derived and the reduced census is accepted.

The terminal retains the oriented-complex wording only through the explicit
implication that failure to map the underlying simplex sets is already
sufficient to rule out a total oriented-complex automorphism. No face or
four-simplex orientation-parity result is inferred from the unordered closure
census.

The finite census does not evaluate the action, an Osterwalder-Schrader
bilinear, Hermiticity, reflection positivity, or an all-volume theorem. It does
not authorize Phase515/516, Phase458, Binder work, sampling, HMC, production,
launch, or a physical-unit claim. `promotedPhysicalMassClaimCount=0`.
Construction remains target-blind and external review remains pending.
