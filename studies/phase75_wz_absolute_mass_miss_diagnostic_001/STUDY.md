# Phase LXXV W/Z Absolute Mass Miss Diagnostic

## Purpose

Diagnose why the Phase LXXIV absolute W/Z predictions miss the physical targets.

## Result

Both W and Z require nearly the same scale increase, about `1.153x`. The required-scale spread is below `0.2%`.

This means the miss is coherent across W and Z. The next likely blocker is the weak-coupling amplitude normalization or scalar-sector relation, not a W/Z-specific shared-scale failure.

## Closure

Do not promote the target-implied weak coupling as a fitted bridge. The next phase should audit the weak-coupling amplitude extractor for missing normalization factors.
