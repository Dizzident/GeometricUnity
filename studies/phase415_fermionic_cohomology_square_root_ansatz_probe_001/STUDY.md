# Phase415: Fermionic Cohomology Square-Root Ansatz Probe

## Question

Can the Superphysics `part-09b` section 9.3 cohomology/square-root clue be
turned into a target-blind first-order complex that produces a welded spin-0
Standard Model doublet or observed boson projection data?

## Method

Phase415 treats `part-09b` as a research clue, not a theorem. It records the
minimum specification fields required for a meaningful `delta_omega` complex:
the differential, carrier sequence, cohomology target, square law,
adjoint/inner product, scalar-doublet projection, observed boson rows, and
normalization lineage.

It then enumerates the locally specifiable candidates:

- chiral Dirac/Yukawa square-root reading;
- VO-7 gauge-compatibility control complex;
- fermion-induced source/backreaction complex.

Each is bound to existing no-go evidence from Phases389, 397, 401, and 411.
The remaining candidates are left open only because they require new source
data: a source-defined `delta_omega` complex or a computable unobserved-phase
carrier.

## Result

The probe passes as a fail-closed negative result:

- no reviewed source supplies an explicit `delta_omega` complex;
- every locally specified candidate is closed or non-promotional;
- no candidate produces a welded spin-0 SM doublet;
- no candidate produces observed photon/W/Z/H projection rows;
- no Phase201 or Phase256 field is filled.
