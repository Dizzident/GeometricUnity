# Phase397: Parametrized u(1) Extension and Neutral-Mixing Underdetermination Probe

## Question

Phase396 left the Phase256 gap at the electroweak embedding. Hypercharge
cannot be derived from the toy branch, so: what does the u(1) extension
machinery look like, what do the sector source channels carry, and exactly
what blocks the photon/Z separation?

## Construction

- u(1) variation per edge: forward block `(i/h) Gamma (x) I_3`, Hermitian
  conjugate backward; fermion u(1) charge `q` an explicit underived
  parameter (su(2)-adjoint matter has no canonical hypercharge).
- Extended per-edge carrier: `n_omega + charged plane + u(1)` with residual
  U(1)_{n_omega} charges {0, +-1, 0} - TWO neutral channels.
- Extended 4x4 block Gram on the converged shell; sector source channels.

## Results (three findings)

1. **The Z-like channel is sourceless**: the su(2)-neutral source fraction
   is <= 0.0023 per mode (consistent with the Phase395 response direction
   being orthogonal to n_omega). The u(1) source channel is nonzero.
2. **The fermion-bilinear neutral mixing element VANISHES identically**
   (ratio <= 4.5e-9, a trace selection rule tr(rho(n_omega)) = 0): the two
   neutral channels are exactly decoupled in this channel. Photon/Z-style
   mixing cannot be generated by fermion bilinears even with couplings
   supplied - it requires a symmetry-breaking scalar/VEV sector, which this
   branch lacks and which is precisely the missing Phase201 Higgs scalar
   source row. This mirrors the SM, where W3-B mixing enters through the
   Higgs sector.
3. **Photon/Z underdetermination**: the separation in the 2-dim neutral
   space is a one-parameter eigenbasis family; no member is target-blind
   distinguishable. The named gap: {hypercharge lineage, coupling-ratio
   lineage, symmetry-breaking scalar sector}.

u(1) extension Hermiticity exact (residual 0).

## Status

Fail-closed. `q` is not physical, no weak angle selected, zero
Phase256/Phase201 fields filled.
