## Summary

The dressing-field method is a structured way to construct composite variables
that are invariant under a gauge subgroup. The route is relevant to the current
boson blocker because it directly addresses how one can extract
gauge-invariant electroweak content rather than treating gauge-fixed elementary
fields as physical by fiat.

The most relevant electroweak lead is the use of the Higgs field as a dressing
for weak-sector variables, closely related to unitary-gauge variables but with
the conceptual distinction that dressed variables are meant to expose invariant
content rather than merely impose a gauge condition. The second-order Standard
Model source provides a nearby electroweak reformulation where fields are
combined with the Higgs field to make SU(2)-invariant variables.

## Limitation

The reviewed sources are external variable-reformulation and interpretation
sources. They show how gauge-invariant variables can be built in electroweak or
Higgs-like settings, but they still start from Standard Model field content,
Higgs structure, couplings, and vacuum assumptions. A variable dressing is not
itself a GU source law for observed W/Z/H pole masses.
