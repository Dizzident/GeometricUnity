## Summary

This cluster tracks BF/BFCG and topological mass-generation routes after the
MacDowell-Mansouri / Cartan-breaking audit. The common idea is that vector
boson mass can arise from topological or higher-gauge couplings, often through
BF-type terms, rather than from an ordinary Higgs potential.

The most direct electroweak lead is Gromov's topological-origin paper. It
states that W and Z masses can arise when the free Lagrangian is transformed
from noncompact matter-field space to a compact sphere. In that model the W and
Z masses are written with the same formulas as the standard electroweak case,
but the free parameter is reinterpreted as the curvature radius of spherical
matter-field space. The source is relevant because it tries to give W/Z masses
a topological origin, but it also removes the conventional scalar Higgs field
and keeps a free radius parameter.

The BF/topological mass papers provide the general mechanism. Non-Abelian BF
models use a B wedge F coupling, sometimes with auxiliary fields, so that
gauge fields can become massive while preserving gauge structure. Savvidy's
four-dimensional tensor-gauge route constructs a Chern-Simons-like invariant
that produces a gauge-invariant mass gap. Dvali, Jackiw, and Pi relate
four-dimensional topological entities to Schwinger-like mass generation. The
BFCG source supplies higher-gauge topological context, not an electroweak mass
prediction by itself.

The no-go source is important boundary evidence. It proves that a
power-counting-renormalizable non-Abelian generalization of the Abelian
topological mass mechanism in four dimensions is not available in the simple
form considered there. Later constructions can add auxiliary fields or alter
the framework, but that is precisely why a GU-local completion must state its
mass parameter, field map, unitarity/renormalizability status, and physical
projection explicitly.

## Prediction Relevance

This route is relevant because it is one of the closest external mechanisms to
a direct geometric/topological mass source: a BF-type term can generate a mass
gap for a vector gauge field without simply writing a Proca mass by hand.

It still does not complete the prediction contract. The electroweak
topological-origin model keeps a free curvature-radius parameter and conflicts
with the observed Higgs sector. The BF and non-Abelian topological mass sources
do not identify GU fields with the required BF/BFCG fields, do not derive the
mass parameter from GU, do not provide observed photon/W/Z/H projection rows,
and do not supply Higgs scalar-source or GeV-normalization lineage.

## Limitation

Treat this as an external topological mass lead, not promotion-ready W/Z/H
evidence. Before it can be revisited as prediction evidence, the route needs a
GU-local BF/BFCG map, a target-independent topological mass parameter, a
renormalizable/unitary electroweak completion, observed-field projection,
Higgs-sector compatibility, weak-angle and VEV or equivalent scale lineage, and
physical-unit normalization.
