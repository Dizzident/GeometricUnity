## Summary

This cluster covers Kaluza-Klein and related extra-dimensional routes in which
internal geometry can generate massive gauge fields. The most relevant lead is
the non-Killing/internal-symmetry route: four-dimensional gauge fields can arise
from internal fields that are not exact Killing isometries, and the associated
mass terms are controlled by internal metric data such as Lie derivatives of
the internal metric. The 2026 JHEP version of the chiral-interaction follow-up
adds a weak-force-adjacent lead by showing that massive Kaluza-Klein gauge
fields can have asymmetric left/right fermion couplings.

The older internal-space-dynamics and coset/gauge-Higgs sources provide
supporting context: internal metric transitions, coset dimensional reduction,
Wilson-flux breaking, and specific gauge-Higgs grand-unification models can all
produce symmetry breaking or mass-spectrum structure from geometry. These are
useful comparison routes because they are direct geometric mass-generation
precedents rather than purely phenomenological fits.

## Prediction Relevance

The cluster is the strongest external lead found so far for a direct geometric
W/Z-like mass source. It supplies the idea that internal metric geometry can
produce massive gauge bosons without adding an ad hoc Higgs field, and it gives
weak-chirality motivation through massive non-Killing gauge fields.

That still does not complete this repository's prediction contract. The route
does not supply a GU-local theorem mapping its internal mass formula into
observed SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y rows, does not provide separate W and Z source rows,
does not derive the weak mixing angle, does not normalize gauge couplings, does
not extract photon/W/Z/H observed fields, does not choose a target-independent
weak scale, and does not provide Higgs scalar-source or self-coupling lineage.

## Limitation

Treat as an external geometric mass-generation lead, not as a prediction source.
It requires a specific internal manifold, internal metric vacuum, stabilization
mechanism, electroweak embedding, weak-scale selection, fermion-spectrum and
coupling matching, and low-energy transport before it can be tested against the
Phase201/Phase256 contracts.
