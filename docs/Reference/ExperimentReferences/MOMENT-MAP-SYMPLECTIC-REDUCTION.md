## Summary

Moment maps and Marsden-Weinstein reduction provide a standard geometric
framework for reducing phase spaces by symmetry. In gauge theory this becomes
closely tied to Yang-Mills phase spaces, symplectic quotients of connections,
and stratified reduced spaces.

The most relevant physics source for the current blocker is Diez and Rudolph:
it applies singular symplectic cotangent-bundle reduction to Yang-Mills-Higgs
theory and explicitly emphasizes the Higgs sector of the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam
model. Other sources add Yang-Mills moment-map context, Yang-Mills-Higgs flow
with symplectic vortex equations, boundary Yang-Mills reduction, and Higgs
branch symplectic singularities.

## How It Was Used

Phase364 audits this route after native GU vacuum-Hessian, global observed
sector vacuum-scan, theta-omega inhomogeneous-gauge, and Hitchin/Higgs-bundle
audits. The purpose is to test whether moment-map or symplectic-reduction
geometry supplies the missing GU W/Z/H source-lineage artifacts or only an
external reduced-phase-space lead.

The result is a negative promotion boundary. The sources provide a serious
geometric route for reduced phase spaces and Higgs-sector stratification, but
not a GU-local source law for observed W, Z, or Higgs masses.

## Prediction Relevance

The route is relevant to:

- GU-local searches for a Hamiltonian or symplectic phase-space formulation;
- moment maps as candidates for gauge constraints or vacuum-selection maps;
- reduced electroweak phase-space geometry;
- singular strata in Yang-Mills-Higgs and Higgs-branch geometries;
- distinguishing reduced phase space from physical pole-mass prediction;
- future searches for a target-independent reduced electroweak vacuum.

It does not currently supply:

- a GU-local moment map or symplectic phase space derived from Shiab or
  observer-sector geometry;
- a target-independent Hamiltonian reduction and reduced-vacuum selection law;
- observed electroweak SU(2) x U(1), photon/W/Z/H projection, and row identity;
- separate observed W and Z source rows;
- weak-mixing-angle and gauge-coupling normalization from GU geometry;
- a Higgs scalar-source operator, Higgs doublet projection, or self-coupling;
- a VEV or equivalent mass-scale source;
- low-energy RG/threshold transport and physical pole extraction;
- GeV unit normalization.

## Limitation

The reviewed route is phase-space and constraint geometry. It can describe
reduced Yang-Mills or Yang-Mills-Higgs spaces, singular strata, and Higgs-sector
geometry, but it still depends on the chosen field theory, Hamiltonian,
potential, gauge group, and coupling data. A reduced phase space is not itself a
target-independent observed-boson mass spectrum.
