## Summary

This cluster tracks Norma Mankoc Borstnik's spin-charge-family theory. The
sources describe a Kaluza-Klein-like theory in d=(13+1) with two Clifford-algebra
spin-connection structures. The route aims to explain Standard Model charges,
gauge vector fields, families, and scalar fields observed effectively as Higgs
and Yukawa couplings.

The scalar-sector papers are relevant because they state that several scalar
fields determine masses and mixing matrices of fermions and weak bosons. The
scalars appear as weak doublets and family triplets, and the scalar mass
eigenstates need not coincide with the fields coupling to W and Z. The broader
sources also discuss two groups of four fermion families, a possible fourth
family, and a stable fifth-family dark-matter candidate.

## Prediction Relevance

The route is relevant because it is high-dimensional, Clifford-structured, and
directly discusses scalar fields, weak bosons, and Higgs/Yukawa effective
structure. It is a useful external reminder that a source law must specify the
actual observed scalar and vector mass eigenstates, not only a broad scalar
sector.

It does not fill the GU prediction contract. The source is not a GU source and
does not provide GU-local d=(13+1) embedding, a two-Clifford connection map,
observed photon/W/Z/H projection rows, independent W/Z source rows, a
target-independent VEV or absolute mass scale, a single observed-Higgs envelope,
or GeV unit normalization.

## Limitation

Treat this as an external scalar-sector and weak-boson-coupling lead, not
promotion evidence. A multi-scalar model that can differ from the Standard Model
Higgs sector does not by itself identify the observed W, Z, and Higgs pole
masses or fill the GU source-lineage contracts.
