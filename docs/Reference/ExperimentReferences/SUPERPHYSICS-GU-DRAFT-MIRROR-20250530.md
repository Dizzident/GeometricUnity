## Detailed Summary

The Superphysics pages are a readable HTML rendering of the public GU draft,
not an independent primary derivation. They are useful as a search aid because
the RSS/index currently exposes twenty-five navigable pages covering the full
draft path.

High-value findings from the expanded pass:

- `part-08` gives the Ship-in-a-Bottle/Shiab contraction template and places
  gauge conjugation by `epsilon` inside the contraction operator. It also states
  that the original representation-theoretic operator-choice notes could not be
  located and would need to be reconstructed from Bianchi-identity criteria.
- `part-09` restates the Lagrangian/Shiab context: the draft says multiple
  Shiab choices remain possible, begins with an Einstein-contraction-like
  operator that annihilates Weyl curvature while preserving/mixing Ricci and
  scalar analogues, and says the author's settled Shiab operator was chosen
  for Bianchi-identity properties but cannot currently be located. This is a
  precise research clue for a Shiab-ansatz reconstruction phase, but not a
  contract-fillable operator definition.
- `part-09b` restates the Dirac-pair/Lagrangian motivation and section 9.3
  gives the relevant fermionic clue: `chi` is said to contain observed
  fermions plus LookingGlass, dark spinorial, Rarita-Schwinger, and other
  matter; `omega` subfields are said to accommodate CKM, Higgs-like soft mass
  fields, Yukawa couplings, and gauge potentials; the mixed spinorial-tensorial
  `Upsilon_omega` is then framed as a possible cohomology obstruction whose
  wedge operator might have a first-order square root. This motivates a
  fermionic cohomology/square-root ansatz phase, but still supplies no explicit
  scalar doublet, observed-boson projection, weak-angle, pole, or GeV law.
- `part-12` restates the GU Lagrangian/Upsilon program: first-order
  `Upsilon_omega = 0`, second-order `D_omega^* Upsilon_omega = 0`, and a
  fermionic variation package involving Higgs-like soft mass fields and Yukawa
  couplings. This is programmatic source context, not a completed electroweak
  mass extraction.
- `part-11` provides the observed-field-content language in terms of pullback
  from `Y` to `X`, structure-group reduction, internal quantum numbers, and a
  qualitative statement that decoupling occurs when no VEV pulls subfields of
  `$` above zero. It does not identify photon/Z/W/H mass eigenstate rows.
- `part-12c` contains a stylized chirality/curvature toy relation with
  `m = R(y)/4` for a Dirac equation in approximately constant scalar
  curvature, plus the qualitative link
  `Cosmological Constant <-> Spinless Gauge Field <-> Fermion Mass`. It does
  not supply an electroweak VEV-selection law, coefficients, units, or GeV
  normalization.
- `part-12d` records the final-method boundary: GU offers algebraic internal
  quantum-number predictions but says quantum-field-theory work would be needed
  to sharpen them into energy scales. Its location table matches the already
  audited Phase402 dictionary: Higgs potential at `<Upsilon_omega,
  Upsilon_omega>`, Higgs Klein-Gordon at `D_omega^* Upsilon_omega = 0`, weak
  isospin/hypercharge in pulled-back connection components, cosmological
  constant as a VEV, and Yukawa couplings as a VEV.
