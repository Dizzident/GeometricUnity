## Summary

This source family treats spacetime and gauge fields as emergent structures
from Yang-Mills or IKKT matrix models. The relevant geometric ingredients are
noncommutative branes embedded in higher-dimensional target space, emergent
four-dimensional geometry, compact fuzzy extra dimensions, and matrix
fluctuations around a brane background.

The most directly relevant boson-source lead is the Steinacker-Zahn extended
Standard Model construction. It presents a low-energy Standard-Model-like
brane configuration in which the electroweak sector arises geometrically from
fuzzy ellipsoids and Higgs fields connect branes. The construction also
contains a second Higgs doublet, right-handed neutrinos, and a singlet related
to Majorana mass structure.

Current IKKT work strengthens the broader matrix-model background by analyzing
weak-coupling quantum spacetime, noncommutativity scales, quantum fluctuations,
and induced/emergent gravity. That background is useful for assessing whether
matrix-model brane geometry could become a future bridge source.

## How It Was Used

Phase361 audits this route after string/M compactification, gauge-Higgs
unification, and finite-NCG discrete-Higgs audits. The phase treats the
matrix-model sources as a serious geometric Higgs lead, then checks whether
they can fill the repository's GU W/Z/H source-lineage contracts.

The result is a negative promotion boundary. The route is external to GU and
does not materialize the specific GU-local artifacts required to claim physical
W, Z, or Higgs masses.

## Limitation

The audited matrix-model route depends on an external brane configuration and a
suitable effective potential. The extended Standard Model construction is not
the minimal Standard Model; it includes extra Higgs and singlet structure,
right-handed neutrinos, mirror-fermion boundaries, and anomalous extra U(1)
factors that require additional handling. These are meaningful research leads,
but they are not the target-independent GU bridge-source law requested for
physical W/Z/H masses.
