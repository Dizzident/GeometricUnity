## Summary

Causal fermion systems define spacetime and fields through operators on a Hilbert
space and a causal action principle. In the continuum limit, the framework is
reported to recover Standard Model gauge fields plus gravity, and it gives
massive left-handed bosonic potentials in the electroweak sector.

The same sources also state the key obstruction for this repository. Higgs-like
scalar degrees can be identified, but the Higgs dynamics and field equations
have not yet been worked out. The monograph also states that bosonic masses,
couplings, and mixing data depend on regularization parameters tied to unknown
microscopic spacetime structure and are currently treated empirically.

## Prediction Relevance

This is a serious geometric/variational source lead. It is relevant because it
goes beyond a numerical relation and attempts to derive the structure of
Standard Model interactions from a nonstandard geometric action principle.

It still cannot promote W/Z/H predictions here. The available CFS sources do
not provide target-independent observed W/Z mass rows, do not solve the Higgs
dynamics, do not supply physical photon/W/Z/H projection or pole extraction, and
do not eliminate the empirical regularization parameters needed for bosonic
masses and couplings.

## Limitation

Treat CFS as a high-quality research boundary, not as prediction evidence. A
promotable boson prediction cannot depend on unknown regularization parameters
or on a Higgs calculation explicitly described as not yet worked out.
