## Summary

The 2021 draft is the central primary source for the current investigation. It
anchors the vocabulary and geometry used by the implementation audits, including
GU-native bundle structure, observer-sector language, Shiab-related objects, and
field-theoretic targets that could plausibly lead to a bridge from geometry to
Standard Model quantities.

The draft has been useful for checking whether proposed boson-prediction routes
are GU-native rather than imported from an external electroweak model. It is also
the baseline source used when deciding whether newer summaries or readable
mirrors are merely search aids or can support promotion-ready claims.

## How It Was Used

- Located GU-native geometric structures relevant to electroweak source-law
  searches.
- Helped distinguish legitimate GU terminology from external Standard Model
  assumptions.
- Supported negative audits that found context but not a complete W/Z/H mass
  derivation.
- Supported Phase371's claim boundary: section 9.3 equations `9.18-9.20`
  compile bosonic and fermionic variations into a mixed Upsilon expression,
  while equations `9.21-9.22` and section 10 frame the deformation-complex
  program. Phase371 implements only a discrete connection-to-Dirac
  first-variation precursor, not that full coupled program.
- Supported Phase372's bounded reciprocal-source experiment. Section 9.3
  equations `9.16-9.20` describe a Dirac-like spinorial operator and compile
  its bosonic and fermionic variations into mixed `Upsilon` terms. Equation
  `10.5` places those terms in the proposed deformation-complex equation.
  Phase372 tests only a local discrete Hermitian-bilinear control candidate.
- Preserved the primary-source caveat: the diagram following equation `10.10`
  is carried over from an older version and may remain inconsistent until
  stabilized. It cannot be treated as a completed mixed-block theorem.
- Preserved Phase373's claim boundary: the local `K`, `A=M_psi^-1 K`, and
  `B=M_psi^-1/2 K M_psi^-1/2` distinction repairs a discrete representation
  mismatch only. It is not supplied by the draft as a stabilized physical
  fermionic branch.

## Prediction Relevance

The draft supports the search space for a direct geometric bridge-source law, but
it has not yet supplied the missing contract fields: separate W and Z source
rows, target-independent VEV lineage, weak-mixing-angle lineage, observed-field
extraction, Higgs scalar-source and self-coupling lineage, and GeV unit
normalization.

## Limitation

Current audits should not promote W, Z, or Higgs masses from this source alone.
The source remains necessary context, not sufficient prediction evidence.
