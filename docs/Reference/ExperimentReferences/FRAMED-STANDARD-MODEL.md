## Summary

Framed gauge theory promotes internal symmetry-space frame vectors, or
vielbeins, to field variables. In the Framed Standard Model these variables are
called framons. The electroweak Higgs is interpreted as an electroweak framon,
and the full framed structure introduces a global generation symmetry.

The FSM literature develops a rotating rank-one fermion mass matrix, a
scale-dependent metric in generation space, and a mechanism by which a QCD
theta term is translated into a CKM phase. Its quantitative tests fit fermion
masses and mixings with a small set of parameters and report broad agreement
with measured fermion-sector data.

The vector-boson side introduces a new vector boson `G` at TeV order. The FSM
gamma-Z-G system predicts deviations from ordinary gamma-Z mixing as functions
of the unknown `m_G`. The closer-study paper also describes a hidden sector and
a scalar complex that includes the Standard Model Higgs.

## Limitation

The quantitative FSM route depends on fermion-sector fit parameters, heaviest
generation mass inputs, an unknown neutrino mass input, and a fudge factor in
the RGE treatment. The gamma-Z-G analysis treats the observed W/Z sector as the
baseline and makes deviations calculable in terms of the free vector-boson mass
`m_G`; it is not an independent derivation of observed W/Z masses. The scalar
complex includes the Higgs, but the audited sources do not derive the observed
Higgs mass target-independently from GU geometry.
