## Summary

Exceptional/E8 routes attempt to encode Standard Model and gravitational
structure in exceptional Lie algebra representations. The source cluster
includes Lisi's E8 connection proposal, Distler and Garibaldi's
representation-theory obstruction, the Octions e8(-24) Standard Model
description, an E8 x E8 pre-gravitation model, and a 2025 modified E8
embedding update.

The recurring positive signal is representation-level structure: Standard Model
gauge algebra, electroweak SU(2) x U(1), Higgs or frame-Higgs objects, fermions,
generations, and gravity/pre-gravitation embeddings. The recurring blocker is
also sharp: these are not physical W/Z/H mass-source laws. They identify fields
or representations, then still require symmetry-breaking choices, Higgs
potential or mass-matrix data, couplings, Lagrangian completion, background or
fermion-mass inputs, observed-field extraction, and physical-unit normalization.

## Prediction Relevance

This is a serious algebraic-geometric unification lead. It is relevant because
GU also aims to organize Standard Model and gravitational data geometrically, and
exceptional algebra routes can expose missing representation, branching, and
field-identification requirements.

It still cannot promote W/Z/H predictions here. The current sources do not give
GU-local E8 branching from Shiab or observer-sector geometry, do not provide
separate target-independent W/Z source rows, do not supply physical photon/W/Z/H
projection, do not derive the weak angle/couplings/VEV/scale, and do not give a
Higgs scalar-source/self-coupling law or GeV normalization.

## Limitation

Treat exceptional/E8 material as representation evidence and blocker diagnosis,
not as mass prediction evidence. A promotable route would need a concrete
GU-local exceptional-algebra embedding and a complete physical mass-extraction
pipeline, not just a placement of Standard Model objects inside E8.
