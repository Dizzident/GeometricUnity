## Summary

This source embeds the Standard Model in Weyl conformal geometry. Its key
relevance for the boson-prediction investigation is the geometric Stueckelberg
mechanism: a Weyl gauge field gains mass from a geometric scalar mode, while the
model also induces Higgs-sector structure and allows neutral mixing among the
photon, Z boson, and Weyl gauge boson.

## Prediction Relevance

The route is relevant because it contains an explicit geometric mass-generation
mechanism and a neutral mass-matrix structure with a Weyl correction to the Z
mass. It is therefore closer to the requested W/Z bridge-source law than a
purely phenomenological fit.

It still does not complete the prediction contract. The audited source does not
provide a GU-local Weyl embedding, direct target-independent W/Z theorem,
separate W/Z source rows, target-independent VEV, weak-angle or coupling
normalization, observed photon/W/Z/H extraction, Higgs scalar-source or
self-coupling lineage, pole extraction, or GeV normalization.

## Limitation

Treat as an external template, not as GU prediction evidence. The route
constrains Weyl-sector parameters from observed Z-mass data and leaves the
required GU source-lineage fields unfilled.
