### Gap 1

**Eric Weinstein, “2013 Oxford Lecture – Geometric Unity”, University of Oxford lecture transcript hosted at geometricunity.org; lecture delivered 2013, transcript posted online 2020.** The load-bearing passages are now easy to cite. First, Weinstein says there are “**many shiab operators**” and that one chooses them “bespoke”; that directly undercuts any assumption that the public GU record contains a single canonical Shiab extraction map. Second, he says the “**quartic Higgs piece**” comes from “the Dirac squaring of a quadratic”. Third, in the same Oxford transcript he says scalar curvature would “**coax the VEV**” out of the vacuum. Those remarks make clear that, by 2013, the public story already pointed towards a **quartic** Higgs mechanism rather than a linear scalar extraction. But the transcript still does not give an explicit field-to-doublet map, representation decomposition of the Higgs candidate, or an equation-level epsilon-conjugation operator. **Target independence:** yes, in the narrow sense that these passages do not consume measured weak-sector numbers; but they also do not yield a computable prediction chain. **Derivation status:** heuristic. citeturn43view0turn43view4

**Eric Weinstein, “Geometric Unity: Author’s Working Draft v1.0”, April 1 2021 draft PDF.** In the audited text I located the core geometric scaffolding for the observerse and chimeric bundle — for example, the chimeric bundle \(C=V\oplus H^*\), its semi-canonical relation to tangent/cotangent data, and the spinor factorisation \(S_g(C)\cong S(V)\otimes S(H^*)\). **Timothy Nguyen and Theo Polya, “A Response to Geometric Unity”, preprint PDF, dated 23 February 2021.** This is still the clearest public objection to the original Shiab presentation. Their abstract-level summary says GU introduces a Shiab operator that overlooks a “**required complexification step**”; they then add that including that step “**precludes having a physically sensible quantum theory**”. Later in the paper they sharpen the claim: the original Shiab cannot be defined without complexification because \( \mathrm{Ad}(P)\not\cong \Lambda^\bullet(T^*U)\) over the reals in the way the presentation seems to require. This does **not** prove a noncompact/full-level no-go for every possible GU completion, but it does decisively show that the public 2020-style presentation lacked a mathematically satisfactory Shiab definition. **Target independence:** yes. **Derivation status:** calculation-level objection; not a complete theorem excluding all later completions. citeturn37view0

**Joseph Thomas Cox, “Geometric Unity I: From Heuristic Proposal to Testable Framework…”, ResearchGate PDF page, uploaded October 2025; updated May 2026.** Cox’s GU I gives a genuine formal definition of Shiab. The key theorem-level export is Theorem 2.4, which states that within the declared parity-even admissible class, the Shiab density is “**unique up to an overall scale**”. Appendix B then identifies the pairing explicitly as a Hodge-type wedge–star scalar, with Theorem B.2 giving the parity-even classification and the table in §B.4 separating parity-even metric-generated terms from parity-odd, orientation-dependent ones. This fills part of the nomenclature gap around “Shiab”, but it does **not** fill your requested Higgs gap: the formalised Shiab is a bundle-valued bilinear density, not a published map from GU fields to an \(SU(2)\) doublet scalar on spacetime. **Target independence:** yes, with respect to electroweak observables; the paper is structural/formal. **Derivation status:** theorem-level for the pairing-classification claim. citeturn26view0turn26view1turn26view2

**Joseph Thomas Cox, “Geometric Unity from First Principles I”, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19800512, April 2026.** This later reformulation is especially important because it **narrows** the claim. Proposition 3.17 says Shiab is the canonical parity-even choice; Theorem 3.18 proves algebraic uniqueness only in **2+1 dimensions**; Corollary 3.19 and Appendix E then say that in \(3+1\) dimensions the pairing remains canonical but is **not unconditionally unique** unless one excludes parity-odd middle-degree terms and related extra structures. The paper also states explicitly that the construction is parity-even because it uses the Hodge pairing and not an “**unstarred epsilon contraction**”. That is strong evidence **against** reading Cox’s Shiab formalisation as the missing epsilon-conjugation Higgs extractor. **Target independence:** yes. **Derivation status:** theorem-level. citeturn34view1turn34view0turn33view5

**Joseph Thomas Cox, “Geometric Unity II: Matter & Symmetry on the Observation Slice…”, ResearchGate PDF page, October 2025.** GU II is the nearest thing I found to a usable electroweak scalar construction, but it is not the construction requested in Gap 1. The paper says, “**We take the following irreducible representations**” and then stipulates a Pati–Salam scalar sector \(\Phi_4\sim(15,1,1)\), \(\Delta_R\sim(1,1,3)\), \(\Sigma\sim(1,2,2)\). It then derives the corresponding Yukawa structures and mass kernels. That is a coherent slice-field model, but the scalar sector is **assumed**, not derived from Weinstein’s original chimeric adjoint, spinor 128, unobserved phases, or an epsilon-conjugation map. So this source documents one way a GU-adjacent programme can proceed, but it does **not** fill the missing extraction map from native GU field content. **Target independence:** no measured weak observables are inserted in the quoted algebra, but the scalar content and VEV structure are exogenous assumptions, so it is not target-blind in your stricter sense. **Derivation status:** calculation-level. citeturn40view1turn28view0

### Gap 3

**Cox GU I, October 2025 with May 2026 update.** The relayed claim that there is a **Shiab uniqueness/classification theorem** is confirmed. **Cox GU I and First Principles I on the parity split.** The relayed claim about separating metric-generated pairings from Levi-Civita/epsilon-built pairings is also confirmed in substance. First Principles I sharpens the point in \(3+1\) dimensions: besides the Hodge pairing \(\langle \alpha\wedge *_Y\beta\rangle_E\), there is an independent parity-odd density \(\langle \alpha\wedge\beta\rangle_E\), so a blanket four-dimensional uniqueness claim is unsafe unless extra exclusions are imposed.
