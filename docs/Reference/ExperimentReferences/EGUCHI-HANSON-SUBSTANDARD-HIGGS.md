# EGUCHI-HANSON-SUBSTANDARD-HIGGS

- The Higgs Mass in the Substandard Theory:

- Source type: External physics paper
- Reference index: [ExperimentReferences.md](../../../ExperimentReferences.md)

## Summary

This source gives a geometric and algebraic interpretation of an electroweak
`U(2)` sector using the Eguchi-Hanson metric. Its scope is the Standard Model
lepton/electroweak-boson/Higgs sector, explicitly excluding chromodynamics of
quarks.

The paper reports the relation `m(H) = m(W) / sqrt(sin(theta_W))` and evaluates
it as a Higgs estimate of 115.3 GeV.

## Prediction Relevance

This is a direct geometric Higgs-relation lead. It is relevant because it is one
of the few sources that gives an explicit boson-mass formula tied to a named
geometric structure.

It still cannot promote W/Z/H predictions here. The relation imports `m(W)` and
the Weinberg angle, does not derive W/Z absolute masses or the weak angle, and
misses the observed Higgs scale. It also does not provide a GU-local
Eguchi-Hanson/U(2) map, observed photon/W/Z/H projection rows, target-independent
scale or VEV, Higgs scalar-source/self-coupling lineage, or GeV normalization.

## Limitation

Treat this as a negative boundary for imported W-angle Higgs relations, not as
prediction evidence. A GU prediction cannot use an observed W mass and weak
angle as inputs and then count the resulting Higgs value as a solved source
lineage.
