## Summary

This cluster tracks a nonstandard Higgs-sector route based on the inverse
problem of the calculus of variations and multiplicative Lagrangians. The
newer source develops a non-additive Higgs Lagrangian that becomes equivalent
to the Standard Model Higgs sector in a large auxiliary-mass limit while
introducing small rescalings of the Higgs mass term and VEV, higher-dimensional
self-interactions, SMEFT constraints, and finite scaling factors for charged
fermion masses.

The paper also reports that charged-fermion Yukawa couplings tend toward a
common value numerically close to the Higgs self-coupling after scaling. The
earlier source applies a related nonstandard Higgs Lagrangian structure to
neutrino masses.

## Prediction Relevance

This is a useful Higgs-sector and fermion-hierarchy lead. It is relevant to the
search because it gives a concrete variational mechanism that changes Higgs
mass/VEV terms and fermion-mass organization without simply adding arbitrary
new fields.

It still cannot promote W/Z/H predictions here. The route imports an external
nonstandard Higgs model, an auxiliary mass scale, finite scaling factors,
observed fermion pole masses, Standard Model gauge/Yukawa structure, and SMEFT
matching. The repository still has no GU-local map to the multiplicative
Lagrangian, no target-independent auxiliary-scale or scaling-factor selection
law, no W/Z source rows, no Higgs scalar-source/self-coupling lineage, no
observed-field extraction, no target-independent VEV/scale, and no GeV
normalization.

## Limitation

Treat this as a current variational Higgs-sector lead, not as GU prediction
evidence. Before revisiting it as a possible prediction source, the repository
needs a GU-native derivation of the multiplicative Lagrangian, the auxiliary
scale, and the scaling-factor law, plus independent observed W/Z/H projection
and physical-unit normalization.
