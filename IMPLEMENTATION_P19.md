# IMPLEMENTATION_P19.md

## Purpose

Phase XIX turns the Phase XVIII fail-closed physical W/Z comparison into a real
attempt at an internally derived physical prediction.

Phase XVIII proved that the repository can run a W/Z physical campaign and
correctly report `blocked` when the bridge from computed internal modes to
physical W and Z bosons is missing. Phase XIX is the work required to remove
that blocker honestly.

The goal is not to force a successful prediction. The goal is to produce one of
three defensible outcomes:

- `predicted`, if internally computed W and Z mode candidates are validated,
  uncertainty is closed, mappings and calibration are validated, and severe
  falsifiers no longer block the claim;
- `failed`, if a validated physical W/Z prediction disagrees with the external
  W/Z target;
- `blocked`, with narrower evidence-backed reasons than Phase XVIII.

## Baseline

Current physical campaign:

- `studies/phase19_dimensionless_wz_candidate_001/config/campaign.json`;
- validates and runs with `--validate-first`;
- produces a physical prediction record with status `blocked`;
- does not promote provisional candidate modes, mappings, or calibrations.

Current blockers:

- W and Z mode records are inactive scaffolding, not generated from internal
  computed artifacts;
- mode-identification evidence is provisional;
- physical mode uncertainty is unestimated;
- W/Z mapping is provisional;
- dimensionless identity calibration is provisional;
- fatal/high falsifiers remain active and block physical claim language.

## Phase XIX Goal

At the end of this phase, the repository should contain a repeatable W/Z
candidate-generation and validation path. The physical campaign should either
advance from a generic blocker to a specific validated `predicted` or `failed`
result, or remain `blocked` with a smaller and more precise set of remaining
scientific gaps.

Phase XIX must not copy PDG values into computed observables or mode records.
External targets are comparison data only.

## Work Items

### P19-M1 Inventory Internal Mode Sources

Status: complete.

Find every checked-in or reproducible artifact that could provide candidate
vector-boson mass-mode data.

Definition of done:

- inventory file lists candidate source artifacts, branch selectors,
  environment selectors, refinement levels, and observable ids;
- each candidate source is classified as usable, insufficient, or unrelated;
- no external physical target table is listed as a source of computed mode
  values;
- blockers explain which missing internal artifacts prevent W/Z candidate
  generation.

Suggested output:

- `studies/phase19_dimensionless_wz_candidate_001/internal_mode_source_inventory.json`

### P19-M2 Define W/Z Candidate Extraction Contract

Status: complete for fail-closed extraction.

Specify the algorithmic contract that converts internal artifacts into candidate
W and Z physical mode records.

Definition of done:

- extraction inputs and outputs are documented;
- particle identity is not assumed from mass proximity to PDG values;
- extraction records include branch, environment, refinement, source observable
  ids, algorithm id, and provenance;
- invalid or ambiguous extraction emits `blocked` records with closure
  requirements instead of provisional predictions.

Suggested outputs:

- extractor model or schema for candidate extraction evidence;
- tests proving extraction cannot use external target values as source data.

### P19-M3 Implement Candidate Mode Generation

Status: blocked by missing usable internal W/Z source artifacts; fail-closed
candidate extraction is implemented.

Generate W and Z candidate mode records from internal computed data.

Definition of done:

- W and Z candidate records are generated by code or a reproducible CLI path;
- generated records point to internal source artifacts;
- generated records are marked `provisional` or `blocked` until evidence and
  uncertainty gates pass;
- candidate values differ from target-table copy-through behavior by testable
  provenance, not by cosmetic numeric changes;
- checked-in study artifacts are regenerated from the implementation.

Suggested touched areas:

- `src/Gu.Phase5.QuantitativeValidation`
- `apps/Gu.Cli`
- `studies/phase19_dimensionless_wz_candidate_001`
- `tests/Gu.Phase5.QuantitativeValidation.Tests`

### P19-M4 Build Mode-Identification Evidence

Status: blocked and enforced fail-closed.

Replace provisional W/Z evidence with a derivation-backed evidence chain.

Definition of done:

- evidence records connect each generated mode to a physical particle identity;
- evidence uses internal observables, symmetry content, representation content,
  branch/environment selectors, and derivation ids;
- evidence remains `provisional` or `blocked` if the derivation does not
  uniquely identify W and Z modes;
- evidence may become `validated` only when the validator can check all required
  selectors and source observables.

Required guardrail:

- mass agreement with PDG cannot be the primary identifier for W or Z identity.

### P19-M5 Close W/Z Uncertainty Budget

Status: blocked and enforced fail-closed.

Estimate the uncertainty required for a dimensionless W/Z ratio comparison.

Required uncertainty components:

- extraction uncertainty for the W candidate mode;
- extraction uncertainty for the Z candidate mode;
- branch variation;
- refinement error;
- environment sensitivity;
- propagated uncertainty for the W/Z ratio.

Definition of done:

- no prediction-path mode has `uncertainty: -1`;
- propagated ratio uncertainty is computed by code or explicitly blocked;
- uncertainty records include provenance and assumptions;
- tests cover uncertainty propagation and rejection of incomplete budgets.

### P19-M6 Validate Mapping And Dimensionless Calibration

Status: blocked and enforced fail-closed.

Promote W/Z physical mapping and identity calibration only after mode evidence
and uncertainty are validated.

Definition of done:

- mapping becomes `validated` only with validated W and Z mode evidence;
- calibration becomes `validated` only with a documented dimensionless
  derivation;
- campaign validation fails if mapping or calibration are promoted without
  matching evidence;
- inactive provisional records remain allowed but cannot open the physical
  claim gate.

### P19-M7 Resolve Severe Falsifiers

Status: carried as explicit blockers.

Physical claim language remains blocked while fatal/high falsifiers are active.

Definition of done:

- active fatal/high falsifiers relevant to the W/Z campaign are listed in a
  Phase XIX falsifier-resolution record;
- each severe falsifier is repaired, evidence-demoted, or carried as a precise
  blocker;
- the physical claim gate still fails if any severe unresolved falsifier
  remains.

Suggested output:

- `studies/phase19_dimensionless_wz_candidate_001/falsifier_resolution_plan.json`

### P19-M8 Run The Physical Campaign To A Terminal Status

Status: complete; terminal status is `blocked`.

Re-run the W/Z physical campaign after each bridge element is updated.

Definition of done:

- reference Phase V campaign still validates and runs;
- W/Z physical campaign validates and runs;
- report status is one of `predicted`, `failed`, or `blocked`;
- if blocked, blockers are narrower than Phase XVIII and map to specific

## Guardrails

- Do not use PDG W, Z, Higgs, or W/Z values as computed source values.
- Do not identify W and Z modes by target agreement alone.
- Do not mark mode evidence `validated` without selector-matched internal
  source observables.
- Do not set uncertainty to zero or inflate it to force agreement.
- Do not validate mapping or calibration ahead of evidence.
- Do not bypass fatal/high falsifiers.
- Do not claim physical prediction from an internal benchmark ratio.

## Plain-English Success Criteria

Phase XIX succeeds if the repository has a reproducible internal path from
computed artifacts to W/Z candidate modes, evidence, uncertainty, mapping,
calibration, and campaign output.

It is a successful Phase XIX outcome if the final campaign still says
`blocked`, provided the blocker is specific and evidence-backed. It is a failed
phase if the campaign turns green by treating placeholders, PDG targets, or
benchmark ratios as physical W/Z predictions.
