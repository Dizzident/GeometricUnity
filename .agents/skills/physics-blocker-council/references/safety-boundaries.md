# Safety boundaries

- Keep the repository authoritative. Re-read live state; never rely on an old
  council transcript as current evidence.
- Use council members for read-only analysis. The root conductor alone may edit,
  launch long work, validate, stage, or commit when the original task authorizes it.
- Never simulate, infer, vote on, or replace an external human ruling. Conditional
  consequences and better review questions are allowed.
- In GeometricUnity, preserve fail-closed gates and
  `promotedPhysicalMassClaimCount = 0`. Lattice-unit quantities are not physical
  masses, and no council consensus authorizes a GeV claim.
- Do not weaken a test, scanner, fingerprint, assertion, or preregistration rule.
- Do not expose secrets or inherit unnecessary environment variables into bridge
  processes. Use the already authenticated local CLI without copying credentials.
- Do not permit recursive delegation. Only the conductor may call the opposite
  model family; delegated participants must not call back or form sub-councils.
- Keep run artifacts under `/tmp/physics-blocker-council/`. Do not write council
  prose into `studies/`, `docs/`, `scripts/`, or `src/`.
- Preserve failed, rejected, contradictory, and withdrawn proposals.
- Stop on bridge drift, unexpected tool requests, model substitution, malformed
  output, missing evidence, approval uncertainty, or an unchanged human-only block.
